NINTENDO

MARKET

SEGMENTATION

Age Segmentation

Children and Pre-teens

Nintendo has a long history of catering to younger audiences with family-friendly and age-appropriate games like the Super Mario series and Pokémon games.

Teens and Young Adults

Some of Nintendo's franchises, such as The Legend of Zelda and Super Smash Bros., appeal to a slightly older audience, offering more complex gameplay and storytelling.

Casual Gamers

Accessibility and simplicity

Nintendo's focus on accessibility and simplicity in its games, such as Wii Sports and Mario Kart, attracts casual gamers who may not be regular players but enjoy easy-to-learn.

Intuitive controls

The intuitive controls of Nintendo consoles, like the Wii Remote and Joy-Cons, make it easy for casual gamers to pick up and play without feeling overwhelmed by complex mechanics.

Gamers

Nostalgia and Retro Gamers

By continuously revisiting classic franchises like Super Mario and The Legend of Zelda, Nintendo taps into the nostalgia of older gamers who have fond memories of playing these games in their youth. The Virtual Console service (available on older consoles) and the Nintendo Switch Online membership offer access to a library of retro games, catering to gamers seeking vintage gaming experiences.

Handheld Gamers

handheld devices

Nintendo's dedicated handheld devices, such as the Nintendo Switch Lite and the Nintendo 3DS (as of September 2021), appeal to gamers who prefer portable gaming options, allowing them to play games on the go. The library of handheld-exclusive titles, including Pokémon games and Animal Crossing, has attracted a dedicated audience of handheld gamers over the years.

Global Audience

localize games

Nintendo's efforts to localize games in multiple languages and release them worldwide ensure that players from different regions and cultures can enjoy their products without language barriers.

Universally appealing content

The company's focus on creating universally appealing content, featuring characters and themes that transcend cultural boundaries, contributes to their success in appealing to a global audience.
